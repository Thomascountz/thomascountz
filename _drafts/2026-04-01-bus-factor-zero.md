## How Fast Can We Type?

It's 2026, and by now we all agree that _typing speed_ was never really the bottleneck in software development. We laugh about it now, but early on, LLMs were dismissed as only being good for increasing typing speed. Some people still tow that line, but it's become a of a meme. We're in the phase now of pointing the finger at code review. _Human_ code review. It's fraught with self-inflicted wounds: miscommunication, inflated egos, and unclear rules masquerading as safety, change management, and quality control. It's always been this way—as a consultant, I've seen code review mean anything from "...this is where we shave our yaks..." to "lgtm. yolo. shipit." 

Whether we're talking about writing code or reading it, one thing is becoming clear: humans are slow, computers are fast, and fast is money.

To make my argument stronger, let's assume I don't mean building a website for your cousin's wedding. Big Business™ is trying to reap gains from AI-Enhanced™ engineering teams, and the question of the hour is: how do we increase velocity? How do we ship faster? Today, code review is in the cross-hairs, and it too, will soon become a meme of yore.
